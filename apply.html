Become a Life Progress Coach

Join the founding pilot cohort of student coaches. Support others, deepen your sense of purpose, and help shape a new model of developmental peer coaching here at Global College.

What is Life Progress Coaching?

Life Progress Coaches walk alongside peers to listen deeply, ask thoughtful questions, and support meaningful forward movement under real-life constraints.

This is not psychotherapy or counseling. It is a developmental, non-clinical coaching approach focused on meaning, direction, agency, and supportive growth.

What You Will Do

Summer Semester: Training & Development

  • Complete the application and a 20-minute interview with the Lead Coach.
  • Participate in a 2-day intensive workshop (16 hours).
  • Learn the LPT framework: Recovery, Renewal, and the 4T model.
  • Engage in real coaching conversations across 5 cases.
  • Review the LPC training materials and provide pilot feedback.

Fall Semester: Practice & Implementation

  • Continue supervised practice with additional individual clients.
  • Participate in group co-facilitation.
  • Attend LPT Labs and guided practice sessions.
  • Attend supervision meetings with the Lead Coach.
  • Submit session records within 24 hours after each session.
  • Participate in ongoing evaluation and feedback.

Pilot Training & Practice Pathway

This pilot pathway outlines the training, practice, supervision, and reflection activities planned for the Summer and Fall semesters. Formal certification will depend on the outcomes of the pilot and the future establishment of the Life Progress Center.

  1. Application & Interview: Complete the application and a 20-minute interview with the Lead Coach.
  2. 2-Day Intensive Workshop: Participate in a full 16-hour training introducing the LPC approach and core skills.
  3. Practice Development: Engage in real coaching conversations with 5 cases and document the learning process.
  4. Supervised Practice: Continue coaching practice with additional individual clients, group co-facilitation, and ongoing supervision.
  5. LPT Labs: Participate in structured practice sessions to develop coaching skills, structural competency, and peer learning.
  6. Competency Development: Continue building relational, reflective, ethical, and practical coaching capacity through experience and feedback.
  7. Reflective Portfolio: Submit a short reflective writing including learning insights, anonymized experience, and emerging coaching philosophy.

Who Should Apply?

We are looking for students who are:

  • Interested in coaching, education, leadership, communication, or personal growth.
  • Strong listeners who are open-minded and reflective.
  • Responsible, reliable, and willing to receive feedback.
  • Able to commit across both Summer and Fall semesters.
  • Open to learning developmental coaching skills in a non-clinical peer support model.

You do not need to:

  • Have all the answers.
  • Have prior coaching experience.
  • Act as a counselor or therapist.
  • Fix other people’s problems.

Pilot Program Note

This is a pilot program. Participants are not only trainees, but also contributors to the development of the LPC coaching model. You will be expected to provide honest feedback, participate in evaluation activities, and help refine the system for future cohorts.
